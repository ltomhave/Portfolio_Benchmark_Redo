Animal Help Now

UI|UX Designer and Project Manager

The Overview:

Coordinate with nonprofit leaders and production team on creating and launching an IOS application for a new arm of the nonprofit. This new arm, Pet Help & Rescue, aims to provide users with a way to quickly save pets in case of a disaster by reaching out to a trusted contacts with the personal information to get to your animals.

Tools Used:
  • Illustrator
  • Figma
  • Github
Project Overview
The Goals:
• Bridge the info distance between pet owners and helpers during a crisis • Help support pet owners rescue their pets during disasters • Increase pet survival rate during disasters
The Challenges:
• Coordinating with a team that though talented are primarily volunteers and work different hours
• Learning curve for design andd developers based on the IOS shift to Swift platform.
• Creating a great product on a limited budget
The Team:
This is comprised of:
• Two UI|UX designers
• One Product manager
• 6 Developers
Current State:
• Currently the application is prototyped and we are trouble shooting any code or ux challenges for the launch with engineers
• Prepping to beta test a MVP product

1  -  Creating a Plan

Requirement Gathering

Upon joining the team, my first step was to conduct a stakeholder interview with the non-profit and project leader, David Crawford.

Initial Wireframes

David had drafted these initial wireframes to help communicate his vision of how the app would work. While they provided a guideline for the initial conversation, I soon found that there was an opportunity to create more content architecture around these wireframes.

User Flows

In order to create a better content architecture, I identified that I needed to be able to identify the full scope content needed. New to mobile applications, I very much needed the input of the dev ops team to be able to understand the flow of product and frames needed.

 

To facilitate this conversation, I broke the app down into 4 main functions and created a user flow around each function. I was able to use this tool to conduct cognitive walk throughs with the whole team to ensure clarity on the flows and deliverables.

Sitemap

Once we had worked through the user flows and quantified the content of the app, I was able to further develop the content architecture by creating the below site map. This provided me an overview of the site structure and what screens needed to be created.

2  -  Developing a Design System

The Design System

As a new brand, the actual design requirements were minimal. I was given free range to craft the visual interface, which presented it's own challenges. The royal purple color was the only required color, which I built the rest of the color palette around. One resource available was Apple's Human Interface guidelines for typography, spacing, and components.

5  -  Hindsight

What Worked

While the product is still in development it is too soon to determine what the big takeaways are. My initial thoughts are that the pros of the project have been working with an amazing and dedicated team who believe in the mission of the product.

What could be Improved

While still in progress, I believe in continued hindsight and self improvement. This view will likely evolve as the product does, but I do see a continued opportunity to improve and streamline and process and communication with the team. To this effect I have implemented a github Kanban project board in order to provide group clarity on what is in work and what is ready to be handed off to the development team.
