1  -  Brand Vision

The Crown & Ivy Girls brand was a private brand launched in 2018 to fill a whitespace within the Belk business for a preppy and colorful kid's brand that would appeal to the moms who bought Crown & Ivy Missy clothing. After a year of success designing for the Missy brand, I was given the opportunity to launch and manage the customer experience of this kid's line.

New to children's wear, my first step was to conduct extensive on the customer including stakeholder interviews within the Belk organization to better understand the information that already existed on children's wear brands and the customer.

I also conducted extensive market research to identify benchmark brands within the kid's sector that aligned with our target customer and the missy aesthetic. The below branding packet is a summary of my research which provides context for the visual guidelines included.

5  -  Hindsight and Next Steps

What worked:

The Crown & Ivy Kids customer wants to be able to put together easy and adorable outfits for her kids. Some of the biggest wins for the brand have been where great product and marketing have come together to tell a cohesive story that sells the customer on the total vision. Our Family Moments campaigns showcase coordinated family outfits that make it easy for the customer to envision her family in them and then purchase everything at once. She also wants to be delighted by the product and feel like it’s a value for her dollar.

Opportunities to Improve:

The Crown & Ivy Kids customer shops us because we’re as unique as her family is and she wants to see the value in the purchase. She’s market savvy and has not been inclined to purchase more basic items that she can get from another retailer who can sell it at a lower price point. Another learning is that kid’s are picky, and parents know it. They can spot right away if something is not soft enough or too uptight or has too many buttons for their 3 year old to do by themselves is not worth the pain to get a child into it. Silhouettes need to be easy to get into, wear, and then get out of quickly to make the customer’s experience of the product significantly better.
